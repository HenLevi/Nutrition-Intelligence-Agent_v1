1. Importance of Hydration

Water is essential for nearly every function in the human body, including:

Regulation of body temperature

Transport of nutrients and oxygen

Removal of waste products

Joint lubrication

Cognitive performance and concentration

Cardiovascular function

Even mild dehydration can negatively affect physical performance, mood, and mental clarity.

6. Sodium and Hydration

Sodium plays a critical role in maintaining fluid balance.

Significant sodium loss occurs during prolonged sweating

Excessive water intake without sodium replacement may lead to hyponatremia

For endurance activities:

Recommended sodium intake: 300â€“700 mg per hour

Common sources include electrolyte drinks, salted foods, and soups.

7. Potassium and Muscle Function

Potassium supports muscle contraction, nerve transmission, and blood pressure regulation.

Food sources include bananas, potatoes, avocados, leafy greens, and yogurt.

Low potassium levels may cause fatigue, muscle weakness, and cramps.

8. Magnesium and Recovery

Magnesium contributes to muscle relaxation, energy metabolism, and nervous system regulation.

Low magnesium levels may increase fatigue and muscle cramps.

Sources include nuts, seeds, whole grains, legumes, and dark chocolate.

10. Overhydration (Hyponatremia)

Excessive water intake without adequate electrolytes can dilute blood sodium levels.

Risk factors include endurance sports, long-duration events with water-only intake, and heavy sweating combined with low-sodium diets.

Symptoms may include headache, nausea, confusion, and in severe cases, seizures.

13. Summary

Hydration needs vary depending on body weight, activity level, environment, and health status.

Balanced hydration supports performance, recovery, and overall health.
